I — The Tag

The smallest part of the fastest workout.

A RyznTag is the size of a quarter. It has no battery and never will. What it does have is a tiny coil of copper wire, an NFC chip, and a single URL stamped into its silicon — the one you're reading right now.

Stick it to the side of a bench. The back of a barbell. The handle of a cable machine. It will outlive the equipment.

Once a tag is paired to a machine, every RYZN user who taps it gets the same exercise — no setup on their end.
II — The Tap

A second-long handshake.

When your iPhone gets within an inch of the puck, it emits a radio field. The field powers up the coil inside the tag, which broadcasts its URL back. iOS surfaces a sheet at the bottom of your screen — same one you'd see tapping any NFC sticker.

  1. Phone wakes the tag. 13.56 MHz radio field powers the coil.
  2. Tag returns its URL. A short hop of data: ryznrise.com/scan.
  3. iOS shows a preview. Bottom-sheet style — tap to open in browser, or in RYZN if installed.
  4. RYZN takes over. The right lift is loaded with your last weights, ready to go.
III — The Lift

A workout that builds itself.

The tag carries which piece of equipment you tapped. RYZN reads it, pulls your last performance from that machine, and pre-fills your weight, reps, and target sets. You haven't typed anything.

Between exercises, tap the next machine. The whole session assembles itself as you move through the gym.
